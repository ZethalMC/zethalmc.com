One Boat

In the realm of Rathnir, Boaterism was an intriguing philosophy that intertwined deeply with the lives of its followers. Beyond its simplistic origins, it had grown to encompass a rich tapestry of belief and tradition, where the six types of boats symbolized different aspects of existence.

Deep within the heart of a sprawling metropolis known as Fjörgyien, the Boaterist community had established a special place of worship: the Boat Tower. This grand structure resembles a tower of Boats. It stood as a testament to the diverse facets of life celebrated by the followers.

At the heart of the Boater's Haven was a legend, one whispered only among the most devout. It spoke of a man named Aric, who had lived during a time when the city was plagued by darkness and discord. Aric, an ordinary oak boat maker, had dedicated his life to crafting boats of exquisite beauty and balance.

One fateful day, as Aric set out to sail on his latest creation, he encountered a terrible storm that engulfed his vessel. Struggling against the tempest, he clung to his boat for dear life as lightning crashed around him. When the storm finally subsided, Aric was nowhere to be found, and all that remained was his boat.

The boat drifted along the shores of Ironhaven, passed down from one owner to the next, each unaware of its extraordinary history. Years turned into decades, and the boat's wood began to age and crack, reflecting the hardships of the city itself.

Then, on an ordinary day, as the boat sat in a humble workshop, its timbers creaking with the weight of time, an unexpected event occurred. A bolt of lightning struck the boat, illuminating the workshop with an otherworldly radiance. When the light faded, there stood Aric, not as a boat but as a living, breathing man.

The city was awestruck by Aric's miraculous return from the afterlife, and he became a living legend among the followers of Boaterism. He shared tales of his time adrift, where he had witnessed the hardships and joys of life from the perspective of a boat. His return was seen as a symbol of the eternal cycle of life and rebirth, where even in the darkest of times, hope could prevail.

Aric spent the rest of his days in Ironhaven, crafting boats that bore his wisdom and the lessons he had learned during his time as a vessel. These boats were regarded as sacred artifacts, and those who owned them cherished the connection to Aric's extraordinary journey.

Boaterism in Ironhaven evolved to include the belief that boats were vessels not only for transportation but for the human spirit itself. They symbolized the resilience of life, the enduring strength of the human soul, and the unbreakable bond between people and their creations. In Ironhaven, the legacy of Aric and his miraculous return continued to inspire generations, reminding them that even in the face of adversity, there was always a chance for renewal and transformation.

The Discovery

This is the second book about Boaterism that Ze Alaric Thalos wrote. It is about Harold Teutonicus-ad Alendir and Saunic Spud discovering the existence of Spirits inside of the Boats of the sacred Boat Tower.

The Discovery

In the world of Rathnir, a new religion was born, known as Boaterism. It revolved around the belief that boats were not just tools for transportation but, vessels imbued with spiritual significance. The six types of boats symbolized different aspects of the world, and their importance was central to this emerging faith.

In the heart of Fjörgyien, a vast, picturesque city in the land of Sirradra, the Boat tower stood tall. It was the epicenter of Boaterism, a place of great reverence for its sheer abundance of boats. The tower itself was a symbol of restoration, for it was frequently destroyed and rebuilt by the devout followers who considered it an act of devotion to maintain the sacred structure. Each boat within the tower represented something unique. The spruce boats symbolized Priests and Heroes, honored for their wisdom and bravery. Oak boats were associated with the common people, representing the backbone of society. Acacia boats were dedicated to animals, recognizing their role in the natural world. Dark oak boats stood for monsters and mobs, embodying the challenges that people faced. Jungle boats celebrated the importance of plants, while birch boats were reserved for those who had transgressed the laws and were seen as criminals seeking redemption.

Saunic Spud and Harold Ad Alendir were the founders of Boaterism. One day, while exploring the Fjörgyien Boat tower, they stumbled upon an astonishing revelation. Through their experiments, they discovered that every boat harbored spirits within them. As they delved deeper into their research, they heard a mysterious voice inside their heads, as if the very boats were speaking to them.

'Finally, someone recognized our existence,' the voice echoed. 'We have been guiding the people for many years, even though they did not know. Please, give us the recognition we deserve. Spread our word across the lands, for we deserve to be praised!'

Overwhelmed by this revelation, Saunic and Harold felt compelled to heed the voice's request. In a moment of divine connection, a sacred book descended from the heavens, containing everything related to the Boat Spirits and their wishes. It was their divine mission to share this knowledge with the world.

Boaterism quickly gained followers across the whole province, Enithica-Novoros. The faithful gathered at the Fjörgyien Boat tower, where they worshiped, celebrated, and restored the sacred structure. They believed that by respecting and understanding the spirits within the boats, they could bring harmony to their lives and the world around them.

The Enithica River in Hierapolis also held a special place in Boaterism. It was here that one of the faith's founders had found an enormous treasure. This discovery was seen as a sign that "what is the boat without the water underneath?" The river became a place of pilgrimage for those seeking blessings and a deeper connection to the spirits.

Among the many spirits that Boaterism recognized, two were particularly notable. Knunkles Spud, believed to be one of the first boat creators, was revered as a founding ancestor. He was reincarnated as the spirit atop the Fjörgyien Boat tower, guiding and protecting the faithful. On the other hand, there was Bob the first, an evil sheep with unnatural powers. After one person, named Sir Sonic, killed him, he was reborn as a birch boat, instead of an acacia boat. This boat embodies the belief in redemption and transformation.

Boaterism continued to grow, its followers spreading the teachings of the Boat Spirits far and wide. In Rathnir, the boats were no longer mere vessels. They were sacred conduits to the spirits that guided and protected the people. As the faith expanded, so too did the recognition of the importance of boats and the world they symbolized.

However, as time flowed relentlessly forward, the fervor of Boaterism gradually waned. The once vibrant faith that had united communities and celebrated the spirits within boats began to fade into obscurity.

Among those who still believed, the spirits within the boats continued to speak to them, albeit in fainter whispers. Knunkles Spud and Bob the first remained symbols of faith, but their stories became more obscure with each passing year.

Boaterism, the religion that had once promised spiritual connection through boats and their spirits, was now a shadow of its former self. Its teachings, once embraced by the masses, were now known to only a handful. The world had moved on, leaving Boat Spirits to quietly linger in the hearts of a few who still believed in their presence and power.

Spirits Vision

In the historical city of Libertalia, known for its thriving civilisation and bustling trade, a curious character named Ze Alaric had set out on an extraordinary adventure. He was going to restore Libertalia in it's former glory, as the city was in ruins, after a deadly desease killed all of the old inhabitants, and the city was disbanded.

Ze was a writer by heart, and his travels had led him to the heart of the province Enithica-Novoros, where he'd heard whispers of the enigmatic Boat Spirits. These spirits were said to show themselves only to the Boat Messiah, and it was rumoured that they held the power to glimpse into the future.

One night, Ze found himself wandering around in past Libertalia, and just looking at everything, as his eyes catched an odd light. It seemed to dance across a large empty field, but Ze didn't know what it was. He asked Sloan, his business partner to take a look, but Sloan didn't see anything. Ze decided to take a look himself, and he figured it were spirits. He began to approach the spirits, because he was afraid they saw him. As Ze approached, the spirits began to shimmer with an ethereal light. They had awakened. Their presence felt formidable and tranquil, like the calm before a storm.

Ze bowed respectfully, realizing he was in the presence of something extraordinary. The Boat Spirits recognized his reverence and decided to share their wisdom with this intrepid writer and adventurer. "Ze Alaric," one spirit began, its voice a soft, melodious hum. "We see in you a seeker of knowledge and a wanderer of the world. You are destined to uncover the forgotten treasures of history and bring tales of the past into the light of the present."

Another spirit continued, "In the city of Libertalia, where merchants and marauders alike converge, your path will intertwine with those who wear masks of deceit. Beware of those who offer power at a heavy price, for not all that glitters is gold."

A third spirit spoke, "But fear not, for your journey will lead you to a guardian, a loyal companion who will stand by your side through trials and tribulations. Together, you shall unlock the secrets of ancient relics and hidden maps."

Ze listened in awe as the Boat Spirits foretold future encounters and challenges. The spirits' voices intertwined like harmonious melodies, painting a vivid picture of the twists and turns that lay ahead. The prediction continued, "You shall bear witness to a clash of worlds and ideologies, as forces seek to redefine the boundaries of freedom and tradition. Remember, it is in unity that true strength is found, and through understanding, bridges can be built."

The Boat Spirits, their ethereal voices carrying a weight of solemnity, continued their ethereal proclamations. "Ze Alaric," spoke one of the spirits solemnly, "we foresee turbulent times for those who stray from the righteous path. In the shadowy corners of Enithica-Novoros, the bandits Sir Sonic and SpecialWizard shall meet a fate unbecoming. Their actions will lead them not to honor or glory, but to a fate far darker. They shall receive the Birch Boat, a symbol of doom, a stark reminder that the course of their actions bears no achievement." The aura around Ze and the Boat Spirits darkened, casting a chilling pall over the field. Ze felt a shiver run down his spine as he absorbed the ominous prediction.

Turning to more promising matters, the Boat Spirits continued, "On the brighter horizon, Saunic Spud, the founder of Boaterism, shall remain a beacon of hope and wisdom. His legacy will inspire generations, bringing unity and understanding in times of strife."

"And High Priest Harold Teutonicus Ad Alendir." another spirit chimed in, "His devotion to our faith shall be unwavering, guiding the followers with unwavering dedication. His words will resonate like the tranquil ripples on water's surface, bringing solace to those who seek it."

The spirits' light grew even more radiant as they focused on another individual, though with less intensity, "Lastly, Priest Biuco Spud, while his path may be filled with more shadows than light, his resilience and determination will forge a vital link between the faithful and the unknown. Through his trials, he shall unlock the secrets of balance and harmony."

As dawn approached, the Boat Spirits began to fade, their glow diminishing. Their final words resonated in Ze's mind, "May you be shielded from harm, and may the spirits watch over you, keeping you safe from the storms of life."

With those words, the spirits vanished, returning to their deep slumber. Ze Alaric left the field, his heart filled with gratitude and determination. He knew that his path would be filled with challenges and mysteries, but with the blessings and guidance of the Boat Spirits, he was ready to face whatever lay ahead.
